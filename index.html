Guide

Click any step below to expand the full details. Everything you need, in the exact order you should do it.

Step 1: Secure your immediate safety

Take a deep breath and make sure you’re in a safe place. If your phone was stolen, move to a secure location and stay aware of your surroundings. Your physical safety always comes first.

Step 2: Lock your phone remotely (if possible)

iPhone: Go to iCloud.com/find → sign in → select your device → Mark As Lost or Play Sound.
Android: Go to android.com/find → sign in → Lock or Secure Device.
This adds a lock screen message and disables Apple Pay / Google Pay.

Step 3: Do NOT call or text your lost phone yet

Calling or texting can alert a thief that you’re actively tracking them, which sometimes causes them to wipe or power off the device immediately. Wait until you’ve completed the critical security steps below.

Step 4: Change your most important passwords NOW

From another device, change (in this order):
• Apple ID / iCloud
• Google account
• Email accounts
• Banking / payment apps
• Any app with saved credit cards

Step 5: Suspend or wipe your phone line

Call your carrier (or log in online) and ask them to suspend service. This prevents fraudulent calls/texts and stops premium charges. You can unsuspend later if the phone is recovered.

Step 6: Enable Lost Mode / Erase Device (only if needed)

If the phone is moving to suspicious locations or you have no hope of recovery, trigger Erase Device. This is permanent — only do it after you’ve secured accounts.

Step 7: Alert your bank and freeze cards

Turn off every card linked to Apple Pay, Google Pay, or saved in your phone. Most banks have a one-click “freeze” button in their apps.

Step 8: File a police report

Even if recovery odds are low, a police report is required by most insurance plans and helps if identity theft occurs later. Bring the IMEI/serial number (find it on your original box or carrier account).

Step 9: Monitor accounts for 30 days

Enable login alerts on every account. Watch for strange logins, password resets, or purchases. Consider a credit freeze if you’re extra worried.

Step 10: If your phone is recovered

Turn off Lost Mode, unsuspend your line, and run a full factory reset before restoring your backup. Change all passwords again just to be safe.

LostPhones.com – Why We Built This

We created this site after losing a phone ourselves — and watching it jump from New York to China in a matter of weeks.

The panic was real. Every hour felt like a scramble to figure out what to lock, what to change, who to call, and what actually mattered.

It took a lot of time, research, and trial-and-error to understand the right steps to protect the phone, the data on it, and our identity.
Most advice online was scattered, outdated, or written for tech experts.

So we built the guide we wish we had.

LostPhones.com is a simple, step-by-step resource that tells you exactly what to do — clearly, calmly, and in the right order — so you can protect your accounts, secure your number, and make the best possible decisions at a stressful moment.

If this site makes your situation even a little easier, it has done its job.
