Full Step-by-Step Guide (with 2025 Updates)

Step 1: Check Find My / Find My Device Immediately

Many phones are recovered because they show up inside the airport or on the plane.

Step 2: Contact TSA Lost & Found (If Lost at Security)

TSA has a central lost & found for each airport. Use this official list:

TSA Lost & Found Directory (official 2025)

Most major airports have dedicated phone numbers and email forms.

Step 3: Contact Your Airline

If you think it's on the plane or at the gate:

  • Delta: 800-325-8224 or delta.com/lostitem
  • United: 800-221-6903 or united.com/lostitem
  • American: 800-535-5225 or aa.com/lostitem
  • Southwest: 888-202-1024 or southwest.com/lostandfound

Step 4: Call the Gate or Lounge

If you remember the gate number, call the airport and ask for that gate's lost & found ‚Äî items are often held there for 24-48 hours.

Step 5: File with Airport Lost & Found

Every major airport has its own lost & found (separate from TSA). Google "[airport code] airport lost and found" for the direct number.

What Usually Happens

85-90% of phones lost at airports are recovered when people contact the right place quickly. Airport staff are trained to help.
